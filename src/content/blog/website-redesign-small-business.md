You built your website three years ago, and it looked great at the time. But lately, something feels off. Visitors aren't staying. Calls aren't coming in like they used to. Your competitor's new site makes yours look outdated. Sound familiar?

A website isn't something you build once and forget. Technology changes, design trends evolve, and customer expectations grow. Here's how to know when your small business needs a redesign.

## 7 Signs You Need a Website Redesign

**1. It doesn't work well on mobile.** Over 60% of web traffic now comes from phones. If visitors have to pinch, zoom, and scroll sideways to read your content, they're leaving. Pull up your site on your phone right now. If it's frustrating to use, that's reason enough to redesign.

**2. It loads slowly.** If your homepage takes more than 3 seconds to load, you're losing nearly half your visitors before they even see your content. Speed matters more than ever, and older sites built on bloated platforms are the worst offenders.

**3. Your business has changed.** Maybe you've added new services, changed your branding, or shifted your target audience. If your website still reflects who you were two years ago, it's not serving who you are today.

**4. You can't update it yourself.** If you need to call a developer every time you want to change your hours or add a photo, your website is working against you. Modern sites should give you easy control over your content.

**5. You're not showing up on Google.** Older websites often lack proper SEO structure. Missing meta tags, slow loading, no blog, poor mobile experience, and outdated code all hurt your search rankings.

**6. It looks dated.** Web design trends change noticeably every 2 to 3 years. A site that looked modern in 2022 can look tired in 2026. Visitors judge your business by your website, and an outdated design suggests an outdated business.

**7. Your conversion rate is dropping.** Fewer contact form submissions, fewer calls, fewer bookings. If your traffic hasn't changed but your leads have decreased, your website's design or user experience is likely the problem.

## What a Redesign Involves

A website redesign doesn't necessarily mean starting from scratch. Depending on what's wrong, it might mean:

- **A fresh visual design** with modern layout and typography
- **A rebuilt site structure** with better navigation and faster loading
- **New content** that speaks to your current audience and includes SEO keywords
- **Mobile optimization** so everything works perfectly on phones and tablets
- **New features** like online booking, contact forms, or an AI chat agent

## How Long Does It Take?

A typical small business website redesign takes 1 to 3 weeks. Here's a rough timeline:

- **Days 1 to 3:** Discussion about goals, content review, and planning
- **Days 4 to 10:** Design and development work
- **Days 11 to 14:** Review, feedback, and revisions
- **Day 15+:** Launch and post-launch adjustments

If you have your content ready (text, photos, logos), the process moves faster. If you need help with content, that adds a bit more time.
