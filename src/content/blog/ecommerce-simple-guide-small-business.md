# How to Start Selling Online: A Simple E-Commerce Guide for Small Businesses

You've got a great product. Your customers love you. But every week, someone asks: *"Do you have a website where I can order?"*

If you're still taking orders through WhatsApp messages and handwritten notes, you're leaving money on the table. The good news? Setting up an online store is easier and cheaper than you think.

Here's a straightforward guide to getting your small business selling online — no tech degree required.

## Why Every Small Business Needs an Online Store in 2026

Let's be real: the way people buy has changed. Even in Guatemala, customers expect to browse products, compare prices, and order from their phone — at 11pm on a Sunday.

Here's what an online store gives you:

- **Sales while you sleep.** Your store is open 24/7, even when you're not.
- **Reach beyond your neighborhood.** Sell to customers in other cities, or even other countries.
- **Fewer repetitive questions.** Product details, prices, and availability are all there.
- **Professional credibility.** A well-designed store makes you look established and trustworthy.
- **Payment automation.** No more chasing invoices or tracking cash by hand.

The best part? You don't need a massive budget or a team of developers to get started.

## Step 1: Choose What You're Selling (And How)

Before you pick a platform, get clear on your products:

**Physical products** — Things you ship or deliver locally (clothing, food, crafts, electronics). You'll need to think about shipping costs and delivery zones.

**Digital products** — Downloads like PDFs, templates, courses, or software. No shipping headaches — customers get instant access.

**Services** — Consultations, classes, bookings. You might need a scheduling tool integrated with your store.

**Pro tip:** Start with your 5-10 best sellers. You don't need your entire catalog online on day one. Add more products as you get comfortable with the process.

## Step 2: Pick the Right Platform

There are dozens of e-commerce platforms. Here's what actually matters for small businesses:

### Shopify
The most popular option globally. Easy to set up, handles payments and shipping for you. Monthly fee starts around $29/month. Good if you want everything in one place and don't mind paying a monthly fee.

### WooCommerce (WordPress)
Free plugin, but you need hosting and some technical comfort. Very flexible — you can customize almost anything. Best if you already have a WordPress site.

### Standalone Website with Payment Links
For many small businesses, the simplest approach is a clean website with product pages and WhatsApp or payment links. No monthly platform fees. You keep more of each sale.

### What We Recommend
If you're just starting out, don't overcomplicate it. A fast, mobile-friendly website with clear product pages and a simple checkout process beats a fancy platform you can't manage.

The key is: **your customers should be able to find a product, see the price, and buy it in under 60 seconds.**

## Step 3: Set Up Your Product Pages

Each product page needs these basics:

**A clear photo.** Good product photography sells. Use natural light, a clean background, and show the product from multiple angles. Your phone camera is fine — just make sure the image is sharp and well-lit.

**A honest description.** Write like you're explaining the product to a friend. What is it? What problem does it solve? What makes yours special? Keep it under 200 words.

**The price.** Visible, in Quetzales. No hidden fees or "contact for pricing" — that kills sales instantly.

**A clear call-to-action.** "Add to Cart," "Buy Now," "Order on WhatsApp" — make it obvious what to do next.

**Product details.** Size, weight, materials, ingredients — whatever your customer needs to make a decision without messaging you.

## Step 4: Set Up Payments

You have several options in Guatemala:

**Bank transfer (transferencia).** Simple and common. Include your bank details at checkout. Customers pay and send you the receipt.

**WhatsApp ordering.** Many customers prefer to order through WhatsApp and pay on delivery or via transfer. This works well for local businesses.

**Payment gateways.** Services like Stripe, PayPal, or local processors let customers pay with card. More professional, but they take a small percentage per transaction.

**Cash on delivery (contra entrega).** Still popular locally. Offer it if you deliver in your area.

**Our advice:** Start with bank transfer and WhatsApp. Add card payments later when your volume justifies the fees.

## Step 5: Figure Out Shipping and Delivery

This is where many small businesses get stuck. Keep it simple:

**Local delivery.** If you're in Guatemala City or Antigua, offer delivery within a radius. Charge a flat fee (Q25-50) or free above a minimum order.

**Pickup.** Let customers pick up from your location. Zero shipping cost for you and them.

**Shipping via courier.** For national delivery, use services like Cargo Expreso or Guatex. Add the cost to your product price or charge it separately.

**Start with one method.** You don't need to offer everything from day one. Local delivery and pickup cover most small businesses perfectly.

## Step 6: Make It Mobile-First

Here's a stat that should shape everything you do: **over 80% of your customers will visit your store on their phone.**

That means:

- Large, tappable buttons (not tiny links)
- Images that load fast (compress them!)
- Text that's readable without zooming
- Checkout that works in 3-4 taps maximum
- WhatsApp button always visible

Test your store on your own phone before launching. If you have to pinch and zoom to read the price, it's not ready.

## Step 7: Launch and Tell Everyone

Your store is ready. Now get people there:

1. **Share on WhatsApp.** Send the link to your contacts and groups. This is your #1 channel in Guatemala.
2. **Post on social media.** Instagram and Facebook posts with product photos and a link to buy.
3. **Add to your Google Business Profile.** Update your listing with your website link.
4. **Tell existing customers.** A simple message: "Hey, we now have an online store! Check it out at [link]."
5. **Ask for feedback.** Your first 10 customers will tell you what's confusing or broken. Listen to them.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Too many products at launch.** Start small. Add more as you go.

**No photos or bad photos.** A blurry, dark photo kills trust instantly. Take 10 minutes to photograph your products properly.

**Hidden prices.** If customers have to ask for the price, most will just leave.

**Complicated checkout.** Every extra step loses customers. Keep it to 3 steps maximum.

**No mobile testing.** If it doesn't work on a phone, it doesn't work.

**Ignoring WhatsApp.** In Guatemala, WhatsApp is how people communicate. Make it easy for customers to reach you.

## You Don't Need to Be Tech-Savvy

The biggest myth about e-commerce is that you need to be "good with technology" to sell online. You don't. You need to know your products, know your customers, and set up a simple system that works.

Start with 5 products. Take good photos. Write clear descriptions. Share the link. That's it. You can always improve and expand later.

The businesses that win online aren't the ones with the fanciest technology — they're the ones that actually show up and make it easy for customers to buy.
