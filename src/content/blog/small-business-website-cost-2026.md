## The Three Tiers of Small Business Websites

### Tier 1: Basic Website ($150 to $400)

This gets you a clean, professional single-page or small multi-page site. Think of it as your digital business card. You get:

- A homepage with your key information
- A services or products page
- A contact page or form
- Mobile responsive design
- Basic SEO setup (titles, descriptions, speed)

This is perfect for a small business that just needs an online presence. A local plumber, a barber shop, a freelance photographer. You don't need 50 pages, you need something that looks professional and tells people how to reach you.

## Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

The sticker price of a website isn't the full story. Here are costs that catch small business owners off guard:

**Hosting fees.** Some agencies charge $50 to $200 per month for hosting that costs them $5 to $20. Ask what hosting costs separately from the design work.

**Domain registration.** This should cost $10 to $15 per year. If someone charges you $50 or more for a domain, they're marking it up significantly.

**"Maintenance" packages.** Some designers lock you into monthly maintenance plans at $100 to $300 per month for work that takes 15 minutes. Make sure you understand what you're paying for.

**Revision limits.** Some agencies charge extra for every change after the initial design. Ask upfront how many rounds of revisions are included.

**Stock photos.** Professional images can cost $10 to $50 each if purchased separately. Some designers include them, others charge extra.

## What About DIY Website Builders?

Platforms like Wix, Squarespace, and Shopify let you build your own site for $15 to $40 per month. They work, but here's what most people don't tell you:

- You'll spend 20 to 40 hours building it (what's your time worth?)
- The templates look similar to thousands of other sites
- SEO features are limited compared to a custom build
- You're locked into their platform (moving your site later is painful)
- The monthly cost adds up: $30/month over 3 years is $1,080

A professional build often costs less over time and performs better.

## The ROI Question

The right question isn't "how much does a website cost?" It's "how much revenue can a good website generate?"

If a $390 website brings in even one new customer per month who spends $100, it pays for itself in four months. Most businesses see much more than that. A well-designed, fast-loading website with proper SEO can become your most profitable marketing channel.

## What to Budget For

Here's a realistic 2026 budget for a small business website:

- **Design and development:** $200 to $800 (one-time)
- **Domain name:** $12 per year
- **Hosting:** $5 to $20 per month (or free with some modern platforms)
- **Content updates:** Your time, or $50 to $100 per month if you hire help

Total first year: roughly $250 to $1,100 depending on your needs.
