"I already have a Facebook page — do I really need a website?"

It's one of the most common questions small business owners ask. And honestly, it makes sense. Social media is free, easy to set up, and your customers are already there. So why bother with a website?

Here's the short answer: **social media is rented land. Your website is property you own.**

Let's break down why that matters — and how to use both together to grow your business.

## The Problem With Relying Only on Social Media

Social media platforms are powerful tools. But building your entire online presence on Instagram, Facebook, or TikTok is risky. Here's why:

### 1. You Don't Control the Platform

Facebook changes its algorithm constantly. One month your posts reach thousands of people; the next month, barely a hundred. In 2026, organic reach on Facebook is estimated at just **2-5% of your followers**. That means if you have 1,000 followers, only 20-50 people see your post.

You have zero control over these changes. Your business is at the mercy of whatever Meta, TikTok, or X decides to prioritize next.

### 2. Accounts Get Suspended or Hacked

Every week, business owners wake up to find their Instagram or Facebook page disabled — sometimes for no clear reason. Recovering a hacked or suspended social media account can take weeks, and some never get it back.

Imagine losing years of content, customer conversations, and reviews overnight. That's the risk of putting all your eggs in one social media basket.

### 3. You Can't Customize the Experience

On social media, your "storefront" looks like everyone else's. You get the same layout, the same profile structure, the same limited options. You can't add a booking form, create a custom portfolio, or build a pricing page that fits your brand.

A website gives you **complete creative control** over how customers experience your business.

## Why a Website Should Be Your Foundation

Think of your website as your digital headquarters. Social media outposts point back to it. Here's what a website gives you that social media can't:

### You Own It — Period

Your domain name is yours. Your content is yours. No algorithm change can take it away. As long as you keep paying for hosting (which costs less than a cup of coffee per month), your site stays live.

### You Show Up on Google

When someone searches for "bakery near me" or "plumber in [your city]," Google shows websites — not Facebook posts. Having a website with proper [SEO optimization](/blog/5-simple-seo-tips-small-business) means you can appear in those search results and attract customers who are actively looking for what you offer.

Social media profiles rarely rank well for local searches. Your website does.

### You Build Trust

A professional website signals that you're a legitimate, established business. According to recent studies, **75% of consumers judge a company's credibility based on its website design**. A clean, modern site with testimonials, photos, and clear contact information builds instant trust.

### You Capture Leads

Your website can include contact forms, email signup boxes, WhatsApp buttons, and live chat. These tools turn visitors into leads — people who actually reach out and become customers. Social media comments are fleeting; a website inquiry is a real business opportunity.

## How Social Media and a Website Work Together

Here's the truth: **it's not either/or. It's both.** But they serve different purposes.

| | Social Media | Your Website |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Build awareness, engage followers | Convert visitors into customers |
| **Content** | Short, visual, conversational | Detailed, organized, searchable |
| **Audience** | People scrolling their feed | People actively searching for you |
| **Control** | Limited by the platform | Full control |
| **Lifespan** | Posts disappear in hours/days | Pages stay visible for years |

### The Winning Strategy

1. **Use social media to attract attention.** Post engaging content, reply to comments, run promotions. Be where your customers hang out.
2. **Drive traffic to your website.** Every social media bio should link to your site. Posts should regularly point followers to your website for more details, booking, or purchasing.
3. **Convert on your website.** This is where visitors fill out the contact form, browse your services, read testimonials, and decide to hire you or buy from you.

Think of social media as the conversation, and your website as the handshake that closes the deal.

## What To Do Right Now

If you're currently relying only on social media for your business, here's your action plan:

1. **Secure your domain name.** Even if you don't build the site today, claim yourname.com before someone else does.
2. **Set up a simple one-page website.** It doesn't have to be fancy. Your business name, what you do, photos of your work, and a way to contact you — that's enough to start.
3. **Update your social media bios.** Add a link to your website on every platform.
4. **Post with purpose.** Every few posts, remind followers to visit your website for more information or to book/buy.
